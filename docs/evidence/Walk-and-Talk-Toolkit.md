## Introduction

The primary responsibilities of health programs and leaders are to build trusting relationships and connect clients to care. The **"Walk & Talk"** program facilitates this through 20-minute sessions providing support on healthy living.

### Goals of this Toolkit:

* Find healthier food options.
* Understand physical activity and blood pressure control.
* Learn the benefits and frequency of preventive screenings.
* Focus on medication adherence, mental health, and stress management.
* **Long-term Goal**: Empower participants to start and maintain their own walking groups.

### Why Walk & Talk?

Walking is safe, low-cost, and requires no special skills.

* **Life Expectancy**: 1 hour of brisk walking can increase life expectancy by 2 hours.
* **Disease Prevention**: 150 minutes/week reduces risk of heart disease, stroke, diabetes, and cancer.
* **Mental Health**: Improves emotional well-being and energy levels.

## Preparation & Evaluation

### How to Prepare

1. **Recruit Members**: Spread the word among clients, friends, and neighbors.
2. **Map the Route**: Choose a safe, central, and easy route for all fitness levels.
3. **Be Consistent**: Set a regular date and time.
4. **Get Organized**: Hold a kickoff meeting to collect contact info and discuss attire.
5. **Maintain Momentum**: Use shared success to keep the group motivated.

### Evaluating Your Program

Evaluation should happen **before, during, and after** implementation.

* **Simple Metrics**: Number of participants or minutes walked.
* **Surveys**: Use feedback to modify and improve the program.

## WEEK 1: Goal Setting

Assess current habits and brainstorm changes. Identify strengths and choose activities you enjoy.

### SMART Goals

* **S**pecific: Who, where, when, why, and what.
* **M**easurable: Include numbers (how long, how far).
* **A**chievable: Set doable goals to maintain motivation.
* **R**ealistic: Practical for your daily life.
* **T**ime-related: Set a timeframe and endpoint.

**Example**: *"Emily will walk after work Tuesday and Thursday for 30 minutes, every week until our next meeting on [Date]."*

---

## WEEK 2: Walking and Physical Activity

Adults should aim for **150 minutes** of moderate-intensity aerobic activity per week.

### Activity Levels

* **Moderate**: Brisk walking, raking the yard.
* **Vigorous**: Running, swimming, climbing stairs.
* **Muscle-strengthening**: Weights or resistance bands (at least 2 days/week).

### Step Goals

The average American walks 3,000–4,000 steps daily. Aim to work up to **10,000 steps** by adding 1,000 extra steps every two weeks.

## WEEK 4: Lifestyle Change

Think of it as an **evolution**, not a resolution.

* **Start Small**: Add 30 minutes of sleep or one extra glass of water.
* **One at a Time**: Don't try to change everything at once.
* **Medication Adherence**:
* Set a routine (e.g., during breakfast).
* Keep original containers.
* Don't stop meds without talking to a doctor.



---

## WEEK 5: Healthy Eating

Focus on nutrient-dense foods and limit added sugars and saturated fats.

### Recommended Daily Amounts (Women 31+)

* **Fruit**: 1.5–2 cups
* **Vegetables**: 2–3 cups
* **Grains**: 5–7 ounce-equivalents (Make half whole grains)
* **Protein**: 5–6 ounce-equivalents (Choose lean options)
* **Dairy**: 3 cups (Choose fat-free or low-fat)

---

## WEEK 6: Healthy Weight

### Mindful Eating Tips

* Check-in: Are you actually hungry?
* Limit distractions (turn off the TV).
* Eat slowly and listen for fullness cues.
* Use smaller dishes and utensils.

---

## WEEK 7: Alcohol and Tobacco

* **Tobacco**: Leading preventable cause of death. Damages lung elasticity (emphysema).
* **Combined Risks**: Smoking and heavy drinking significantly increase blood pressure (above 140/90 mmHg).
* **Liver Health**: Chronic drinking can lead to cirrhosis (liver deterioration).

---

## WEEK 8: Mental Health

Mental and physical health are deeply linked.

* **Exercise**: Releases endorphins that improve mood.
* **Immune System**: Depression can weaken your body's ability to fight sickness.
* **Heart Health**: Intense anger or anxiety can increase heart attack risk significantly in the hours following an episode.

---

## WEEK 9: Staying Connected

Chronic loneliness is associated with cardiovascular disease and cognitive decline.

* **Prioritize Socializing**: Schedule time for friends just like you schedule exercise.
* **Hobbies**: Group classes or book clubs create natural bonds.
* **Shared Housing**: Consider roommates to combat the risks of living alone.

---

## WEEK 10: Stress and Sleep

* **Sleep**: Most adults need **7–8 hours**. Lack of sleep causes pessimism and reduces problem-solving ability.
* **Stress Management**:
* Look at the "big picture."
* Accept what you cannot control.
* Manage time effectively.
